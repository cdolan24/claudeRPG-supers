# COMPLETE SKILLS LIST v4.0

---

## SKILLS OVERVIEW

Skills represent your character's training, knowledge, and expertise in various areas. When you attempt a task, you roll d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient).

**Proficiency Bonus by Level:**
- Levels 1-4: +2
- Levels 5-8: +3
- Levels 9-12: +4
- Levels 13-16: +5
- Levels 17-20: +6

**Skill Check Formula:** d20 + Ability Modifier + Proficiency Bonus (if proficient)

**Expertise:** Some class features grant "expertise" - you add **double your proficiency bonus** instead of normal proficiency bonus.

## GENERAL SKILLS

These are the core skills available to all characters. Your class determines how many you can select at character creation.

---

### PERCEPTION AND INVESTIGATION SKILLS

#### **SCIENCE (Intelligence)**
Immediate understanding of scientific principles, technology, and empirical knowledge.

**Use For:**
- Identifying unknown technology
- Understanding scientific phenomena
- Analyzing chemical compounds
- Operating advanced equipment
- Recognizing tech-based powers
- Disabling technological threats
- Building or repairing devices (with Machinery)
- Understanding medical/biological science

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Recognize common technology (smartphone, computer)
- **DC 12:** Understand basic scientific principle (gravity, electricity)
- **DC 15:** Identify advanced technology (alien device, experimental weapon)
- **DC 18:** Analyze superhuman power with scientific basis (radiation, mutation)
- **DC 20:** Understand cutting-edge theoretical science
- **DC 25:** Comprehend impossible/alien science

**Example Uses:**
- "What is this device and what does it do?"
- "How can I counteract this radioactive contamination?"
- "Is this person's power based on quantum physics or something else?"
- "Can I jury-rig this alien weapon to work?"

**Synergizes With:** Machinery (for building), Medicine (for biology), Investigation (for analysis)

---

#### **MYSTICISM (Intelligence or Wisdom)**
Immediate understanding of supernatural, magical, and extranormal phenomena. This skill covers both arcane magic and religious/divine power.

**Use For:**
- Identifying magical effects
- Understanding mystical creatures
- Recognizing divine/infernal entities
- Analyzing spell components
- Detecting curses or enchantments
- Understanding planar mechanics
- Knowing religious lore and rituals
- Identifying magical artifacts

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Recognize common magical effect (detect magic, simple illusion)
- **DC 12:** Identify basic mystical creature (ghost, imp)
- **DC 15:** Understand complex magical ritual
- **DC 18:** Identify rare magical artifact or ancient spell
- **DC 20:** Comprehend divine/cosmic magic
- **DC 25:** Understand reality-warping or time magic

**Example Uses:**
- "What kind of magic is this villain using?"
- "How do I break this curse?"
- "What deity does this symbol represent?"
- "Is this artifact safe to touch?"

**Synergizes With:** Investigation (for research), Insight (for divine understanding), Perception (for detecting magic)

---

#### **INVESTIGATION (Intelligence)**
Logical deduction, research, pattern recognition, and analytical thinking.

**Use For:**
- Searching for clues at crime scene
- Researching information in library/database
- Decoding ciphers and puzzles
- Finding hidden patterns
- Forensic analysis
- Logical deduction (Sherlock Holmes style)
- Discovering secret passages
- Analyzing evidence

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Find obvious clue at crime scene
- **DC 12:** Research well-documented historical fact
- **DC 15:** Decode simple cipher or puzzle
- **DC 18:** Make complex deduction from minimal evidence
- **DC 20:** Solve intricate mystery or conspiracy
- **DC 25:** Deduce near-impossible connection

**Example Uses:**
- "I search the room for clues about where the villain went"
- "I research this ancient symbol in the library"
- "I try to figure out the pattern in these crimes"
- "I analyze the ransom note for information about the kidnapper"

**Synergizes With:** Perception (for finding clues), Science (for forensics), Insight (for deduction)

**Investigation vs Perception:**
- **Perception:** You notice something (passive awareness, quick glance)
- **Investigation:** You analyze and understand what you found (active analysis, deep thinking)

---

#### **PERCEPTION (Wisdom)**
Awareness of your surroundings using your normal senses (sight, hearing, smell, touch, taste).

**Use For:**
- Noticing hidden enemies
- Hearing subtle sounds
- Spotting important details
- Detecting ambushes
- Passive awareness (GM rolls secretly)
- Tracking by sight
- Reading body language (with Insight)
- Noticing when something is "off"

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Notice something obvious in plain sight
- **DC 12:** Hear conversation through door
- **DC 15:** Spot hidden enemy in shadows
- **DC 18:** Notice tiny detail (scratch on wall, faint smell)
- **DC 20:** Detect nearly invisible threat
- **DC 25:** Sense danger before it happens (near-supernatural awareness)

**Example Uses:**
- "Do I hear anyone on the other side of this door?"
- "I scan the rooftops for snipers"
- "Do I notice anything suspicious about this person?"
- "Can I tell if we're being followed?"

**Passive Perception:** GM uses your Passive Perception score (10 + WIS modifier + Prof bonus if proficient) to determine if you notice things without actively looking.

**Synergizes With:** Investigation (to analyze what you see), Insight (to understand meaning), Vigilance (to maintain awareness)

**Note:** Extrasensory perception (telepathy, danger sense, etc.) requires specific advantages - this skill only covers normal senses.

### PHYSICAL SKILLS

#### **VIGILANCE (Constitution)**
Endurance, stamina, resisting exhaustion, and maintaining focus over long periods.

**Use For:**
- Staying awake during all-night stakeout
- Resisting exhaustion from forced march
- Maintaining concentration during long task
- Enduring harsh environmental conditions
- Fighting off fatigue
- Holding breath for extended time
- Running long distances without tiring
- Resisting torture

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Stay awake during boring task
- **DC 12:** Endure minor discomfort (heat, cold, hunger)
- **DC 15:** Stay alert all night without sleep
- **DC 18:** Endure extreme conditions (desert, arctic)
- **DC 20:** Resist torture without breaking
- **DC 25:** Superhuman endurance feat

**Example Uses:**
- "Can I stay awake to watch for the villain's return?"
- "I try to resist the exhaustion from running all day"
- "Do I maintain concentration despite the pain?"

**Synergizes With:** Athletics (for long-distance running), Perception (for staying alert)

**Vigilance vs Constitution Saves:**
- **CON Save:** Immediate resistance (resist poison, avoid spell effect)
- **Vigilance:** Long-term endurance (stay awake, resist fatigue over time)

---

#### **ATHLETICS (Strength)**
Physical prowess: climbing, jumping, swimming, running, and feats of strength.

**Use For:**
- Climbing walls, ropes, or cliffs
- Jumping across gaps or up to ledges
- Swimming through rough water
- Breaking down doors
- Sprinting at top speed
- Wrestling and grappling
- Lifting heavy objects
- Catching yourself when falling

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Climb rope with knots
- **DC 12:** Jump across 10 ft gap
- **DC 15:** Climb smooth wall with equipment
- **DC 18:** Swim through rapids or storm
- **DC 20:** Climb sheer surface or make impossible leap
- **DC 25:** Superhuman physical feat

**Example Uses:**
- "I climb up the building's fire escape"
- "I try to jump from this rooftop to the next one"
- "I swim through the flooded tunnel"
- "I attempt to break down the reinforced door"

**Synergizes With:** Acrobatics (for parkour), Vigilance (for endurance running)

---

#### **ACROBATICS (Dexterity)**
Balance, tumbling, parkour, contortion, and agile movement.

**Use For:**
- Maintaining balance on narrow surfaces
- Tumbling through enemy's space
- Landing safely from falls
- Escaping grapples and restraints
- Parkour and freerunning
- Contorting through tight spaces
- Dodging environmental hazards
- Performing flips and aerial maneuvers

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Walk across wide beam
- **DC 12:** Tumble through enemy's space
- **DC 15:** Balance on narrow ledge
- **DC 18:** Land safely from 20 ft fall
- **DC 20:** Navigate through swinging blades/obstacles
- **DC 25:** Superhuman acrobatic feat

**Example Uses:**
- "I backflip off the ledge and land on the floor below"
- "I try to squeeze through the narrow ventilation shaft"
- "I run across the tightrope between buildings"
- "I attempt to slip out of these handcuffs"

**Synergizes With:** Athletics (for running parkour), Stealth (for silent movement)

**Acrobatics vs DEX Saves:**
- **DEX Save:** React to sudden danger (dodge fireball, avoid explosion)
- **Acrobatics:** Planned agile movement (deliberate parkour, careful balance)

---

#### **STEALTH (Dexterity)**
Moving silently and staying hidden from detection.

**Use For:**
- Hiding from enemies
- Moving silently
- Sneaking past guards
- Picking pockets
- Shadowing someone without being noticed
- Camouflaging yourself
- Blending into crowd
- Stalking prey

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Hide in heavy forest or shadows
- **DC 12:** Move quietly on soft ground
- **DC 15:** Sneak past alert guard
- **DC 18:** Hide in plain sight (crowd, minimal cover)
- **DC 20:** Become virtually invisible to normal observation
- **DC 25:** Sneak past superhuman senses

**Example Uses:**
- "I hide behind the corner and wait for the guard to pass"
- "I try to sneak across the creaky wooden floor"
- "I follow the suspect without them noticing"
- "I pickpocket the key from the guard"

**Contested Checks:** Your Stealth vs their Perception to see if they spot you

**Synergizes With:** Acrobatics (for silent movement), Perception (to spot observers)

**Note:** Certain disadvantages (Heavy Armor) give disadvantage on Stealth checks

### MISCELLANEOUS AND CHARISMA SKILLS

#### **PERSUASION (Charisma)**
Convincing others through honest argument, negotiation, or appeal to emotions.

**Use For:**
- Negotiating deals or truces
- Convincing someone to help you
- Making logical arguments
- Inspiring or rallying allies
- Calming angry person through reason
- Seducing or flirting
- Making good impression
- Diplomacy and conflict resolution

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Convince friendly person of obvious truth
- **DC 12:** Negotiate fair deal
- **DC 15:** Convince skeptical person
- **DC 18:** Persuade hostile person to listen
- **DC 20:** Convince enemy to switch sides
- **DC 25:** Inspire mass movement with single speech

**Example Uses:**
- "I try to convince the gang leader to surrender peacefully"
- "I negotiate with the villain for the hostages' release"
- "I give an inspiring speech to rally the citizens"
- "I convince the witness to testify"

**Synergizes With:** Insight (to read target), Deception (for manipulation)

**Persuasion vs Deception vs Intimidation:**
- **Persuasion:** Honest appeal, reason, emotion (they believe because you're right)
- **Deception:** Lies, misdirection, manipulation (they believe because you tricked them)
- **Intimidation:** Threats, fear, coercion (they comply because they're afraid)

---

#### **INTIMIDATION (Charisma or Strength)**
Influencing others through threats, fear, physical presence, or displays of power.

**Use For:**
- Threatening enemies into surrender
- Extracting information through fear
- Making yourself look dangerous
- Breaking someone's will
- Causing panic or routing enemies
- Displaying dominance
- Forcing compliance
- Scaring away lesser threats

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Intimidate common thug
- **DC 12:** Force information from frightened witness
- **DC 15:** Scare trained soldier
- **DC 18:** Intimidate dangerous criminal
- **DC 20:** Break hardened villain's resolve
- **DC 25:** Terrify supervillain into surrender

**Example Uses:**
- "I grab the informant by the collar and demand answers"
- "I try to scare the muggers away with a demonstration of my powers"
- "I interrogate the captured henchman"
- "I stand tall and growl menacingly at the gang"

**Synergizes With:** Performance (for dramatic effect), Insight (to find fears)

**STR-based Intimidation:** If you're using physical presence and displays of strength rather than verbal threats, use STR instead of CHA (GM decides).

---

#### **DECEPTION (Charisma)**
Lying, misdirection, disguise, acting, and manipulation. Also covers performance art.

**Use For:**
- Lying convincingly
- Creating false identities
- Disguising yourself
- Acting/performing
- Bluffing in social situations
- Fast-talking your way out of trouble
- Feigning emotions or intentions
- Misdirection and sleight of hand (social)
- Faking documents or credentials
- Impersonating someone

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Tell plausible lie to gullible person
- **DC 12:** Maintain false identity briefly
- **DC 15:** Lie to perceptive person
- **DC 18:** Create elaborate deception
- **DC 20:** Fool expert or fool people who know you well
- **DC 25:** Maintain perfect deep cover for years

**Example Uses:**
- "I pretend to be a security guard to get past the checkpoint"
- "I lie about where I was last night"
- "I act like I belong here and walk confidently past the guards"
- "I disguise myself as the villain to infiltrate their base"

**Contested Checks:** Your Deception vs their Insight to see if they believe you

**Synergizes With:** Performance (for acting), Persuasion (for believability), Insight (to know what they want to hear)

**Deception for Performance:**
- Acting on stage or screen
- Playing musical instrument for audience
- Public speaking or storytelling
- Entertainment and showmanship

---

#### **INSIGHT (Wisdom)**
Reading people's emotions, detecting lies, understanding motivations, and social awareness.

**Use For:**
- Detecting lies and deception
- Reading body language
- Sensing someone's true emotions
- Understanding motivations
- Predicting behavior
- Empathy and emotional intelligence
- Knowing when to trust someone
- Spotting tells in negotiation
- Mass insight (see below)

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Notice obvious emotion (anger, joy)
- **DC 12:** Detect poorly-told lie
- **DC 15:** Read subtle body language
- **DC 18:** Detect expert deception
- **DC 20:** Understand complex motivations
- **DC 25:** See through perfect poker face

**Example Uses:**
- "Is the suspect telling the truth?"
- "What is the villain really after?"
- "Can I read the crowd's mood?"
- "Do I sense that the informant is holding something back?"

**Contested Checks:** Your Insight vs their Deception to detect lies

**Synergizes With:** Perception (to notice details), Persuasion (to know how to appeal)

**STREETWISE (Mass Insight):**
Insight can also function as "Streetwise" - understanding the general mood, rumors, and information on the streets.

**Streetwise Uses:**
- "What's the word on the street about the new gang?"
- "Where can I find information in this neighborhood?"
- "I try to get a sense of the criminal underworld's mood"
- "Do people here know about the recent disappearances?"

This is essentially Insight applied to communities rather than individuals.

#### **MACHINERY (Intelligence)**
Operating, understanding, and using technological devices and vehicles.

**Use For:**
- Operating unfamiliar technology
- Driving/piloting vehicles expertly
- Using specialized equipment
- Disabling security systems
- Hacking computers (basic)
- Repairing mechanical devices (with tools)
- Jury-rigging technology
- Understanding how device works immediately

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Operate common device (car, computer)
- **DC 12:** Drive vehicle in chase
- **DC 15:** Use specialized equipment (alien tech)
- **DC 18:** Hack secure system
- **DC 20:** Operate completely foreign technology
- **DC 25:** Use impossible/alien technology

**Example Uses:**
- "I try to hot-wire the car"
- "I hack into the security system to disable the cameras"
- "I pilot the helicopter to chase the villain"
- "I figure out how to use this alien weapon"

**Synergizes With:** Science (for understanding), Investigation (for figuring out controls)

**Machinery vs Science:**
- **Science:** Understanding how/why it works (theory, design)
- **Machinery:** Actually using it (practical, operation)

#### **MEDICINE AND SURVIVAL (Wisdom)**
Medical knowledge, first aid, survival skills, and natural awareness.

**Use For:**
- **Medicine:**
  - Stabilizing dying creatures
  - Diagnosing illnesses
  - Treating injuries
  - Performing surgery
  - Identifying poisons
  - Understanding anatomy
  - Providing long-term care
  
- **Survival:**
  - Finding food and water
  - Tracking creatures
  - Navigating wilderness
  - Predicting weather
  - Building shelter
  - Avoiding natural hazards
  - Living off the land
  - Understanding animal behavior

**Example DCs:**
- **DC 10:** Stabilize dying person with medical kit
- **DC 12:** Diagnose common illness or find water source
- **DC 15:** Treat serious wound or track creature through forest
- **DC 18:** Perform emergency surgery or survive in extreme environment
- **DC 20:** Treat superhuman injuries or track invisible creature
- **DC 25:** Miraculous medical feat or survive impossible conditions

**Example Uses:**
- "I try to stop the bleeding and stabilize the wounded civilian"
- "I diagnose what poison was used"
- "I search for edible plants and clean water"
- "I track the creature through the woods"
- "I predict when this storm will hit"

**Synergizes With:** Science (for advanced medicine), Perception (for tracking), Vigilance (for survival endurance)

**Combat Medicine:**
- **Stabilize Dying Creature:** Action, DC 10 Medicine check
- **Restore HP (with Medical Kit):** Action, 1 use of kit, DC 12, restore 1d6+4 HP

## SPECIALIZED SKILLS

These are additional skills that are more specific and situational. They're not part of the standard skill list, but GMs may allow characters to be proficient in them if it fits their concept.

#### **VEHICLE COMBAT (Dexterity or Intelligence)**
Fighting while piloting vehicles or using vehicles as weapons.

**Use For:**
- Vehicular chase scenes
- Ramming or using vehicles as weapons
- Aerial dogfights
- Evasive driving under fire
- Shooting while driving

**When To Use:** During vehicle chases or combat

**Default:** Use Machinery skill with disadvantage if not proficient

---

#### **HEAVY WEAPONS (Strength)**
Using mounted weapons, artillery, rocket launchers, and other heavy ordnance.

**Use For:**
- Operating turrets or mounted guns
- Firing rocket launchers
- Using artillery
- Wielding superheavy weapons

**When To Use:** When using weapons marked "Heavy Weapons" instead of normal weapons

**Default:** Use STR check with disadvantage if not proficient

## SKILL CHALLENGES

Sometimes a task requires multiple skills used together. The GM may call for a **skill challenge** where the party must succeed on multiple checks using different skills.

**Example Skill Challenge: Infiltrate Villain's Base**
- Stealth to sneak past guards
- Machinery to disable security cameras
- Acrobatics to navigate laser grid
- Deception to bluff past checkpoint
- Investigation to find target
- Success requires 4/5 checks passed

## TAKING 10 AND TAKING 20 (OPTIONAL RULES)

**Taking 10:**
If you're not in danger or distracted, you can "take 10" - automatically get result of 10 + your modifiers instead of rolling.

**When To Use:** Routine tasks with no pressure

**Taking 20:**
If you have unlimited time and no consequences for failure, you can "take 20" - automatically get result of 20 + your modifiers, but it takes 20 times as long.

**When To Use:** Searching room thoroughly, picking lock with no time limit

**When You Can't Take 20:** Combat, timed situations, consequences for failure (disarming bomb)

## GROUP CHECKS

When entire party attempts same task, there are two options:

**OPTION 1: Everyone Rolls**
- If at least half the group succeeds, the group succeeds
- Example: Party sneaking past guards

**OPTION 2: Leader Rolls with Help**
- One character leads, others use Help action
- Leader gets advantage
- Example: One person negotiating while others provide support

---

## ADVANTAGE AND DISADVANTAGE ON SKILLS

**Advantage:** Roll 2d20, take higher result
**Disadvantage:** Roll 2d20, take lower result

**Common Sources of Advantage:**
- Ally uses Help action
- Optimal tools or equipment
- Favorable circumstances
- Class features or abilities

**Common Sources of Disadvantage:**
- Poor conditions (darkness, noise, distraction)
- Inadequate tools
- Wounded or exhausted
- Armor restrictions (Stealth in heavy armor)

## PASSIVE SKILLS

Some skills can be used **passively** - the GM uses your passive score instead of asking for a roll.

**Passive Score = 10 + your skill modifier**

**Common Passive Skills:**
- **Passive Perception:** Do you notice things without looking? (Most common)
- **Passive Insight:** Do you sense deception without actively analyzing?
- **Passive Investigation:** Do you notice patterns without consciously thinking?

**When GM Uses Passive:** Secret rolls where telling you to roll would give information away.
