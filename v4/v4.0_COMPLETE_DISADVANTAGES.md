# COMPLETE DISADVANTAGES LIST v4.0

---

## DISADVANTAGES PHILOSOPHY

Disadvantages provide **negative CP** that you can use to buy more advantages and abilities. They represent flaws, limitations, and challenges your character must overcome.

**Rules:**
- You can take disadvantages worth up to **-80 CP** (GM may allow more for special concepts)
- Disadvantages should actually impact gameplay (GM enforces them)
- You must roleplay your disadvantages appropriately
- CP gained from disadvantages can be spent on anything

**Format:** Disadvantage Name [Negative CP Value]
- Description
- Examples

---

## PHYSICAL DISADVANTAGES

### IMPAIRED SENSES [Variable]

**BLIND [-30 CP]**
- Cannot see at all
- All sight-based actions automatically fail
- Attacks at disadvantage unless you have alternate sense (Blindsight, etc.)
- Can be offset by Blindsight or similar advantages
- **Examples:** Daredevil (but has Blindsight), Toph (Avatar)

**DEAF [-15 CP]**
- Cannot hear anything
- Automatically fail hearing-based Perception checks
- Vulnerable to surprise from behind
- Cannot speak clearly (separate disadvantage)

**MUTE [-15 CP]**
- Cannot speak
- Must communicate via sign language, writing, telepathy, etc.
- Cannot cast spells with verbal components
- Can still make sounds (grunts, screams)

**ONE EYE [-10 CP]**
- Missing one eye
- -2 to ranged attack rolls
- No depth perception
- Disadvantage on Perception checks requiring sight

**ONE ARM [-15 CP]**
- Missing one arm or hand
- Cannot use two-handed weapons or items
- Disadvantage on actions requiring two hands (climbing, grappling)
- Can get prosthetic (doesn't remove disadvantage)

**ONE LEG/LAME [-20 CP]**
- Missing leg or can't use it properly
- Speed reduced to 20 ft (can't run)
- Disadvantage on DEX saves and Athletics checks
- Cannot take Dash action
- **Examples:** Professor X (wheelchair)

**COLOR BLIND [-5 CP]**
- Cannot distinguish colors
- Disadvantage on tasks requiring color identification
- Some magical effects may be harder to notice

### CHRONIC CONDITIONS [Variable]

**CHRONIC PAIN [-10 to -20 CP]**
- Constant pain from old injury or condition
- **Mild [-10 CP]:** Disadvantage on concentration checks
- **Severe [-20 CP]:** Also disadvantage on initiative, -5 ft speed
- Must make CON save DC 12 when taking damage or lose concentration
- **Examples:** Moon Knight, Cable

**ADDICTION [-10 to -30 CP]**
- Must consume specific substance regularly
- **Mild (coffee, cigarettes) [-10 CP]:** Need fix daily, disadvantage if more than 1 day without
- **Moderate (alcohol, prescription drugs) [-15 CP]:** Need fix every 12 hours, increasingly severe penalties
- **Severe (hard drugs) [-30 CP]:** Need fix every 6 hours, incapacitated without it
- Without substance: Disadvantage on all rolls, -2 to all stats (cumulative per day)
- **Examples:** John Constantine (smoking), Iron Man (alcoholic - recovered)

**DEPENDENCY [-20 to -40 CP]**
- Must absorb/consume specific thing or weaken/die
- Not a vice, but a biological need
- **Daily (sunlight, water, etc.) [-20 CP]:** Need 1 hour exposure per day
- **Hourly (fire energy, electricity) [-30 CP]:** Need exposure every hour
- **Constant (magic aura, oxygen) [-40 CP]:** Need continuous exposure
- Without source: Lose 1 HP per hour (daily), 1 HP per minute (hourly), 1 HP per round (constant)
- **Examples:** Venom (must bond with host), Electro (needs electricity)

**HEMOPHILIA [-20 CP]**
- Blood doesn't clot properly
- Whenever you take damage, also gain 1 level of exhaustion
- Healing effects only restore half HP for you
- Death saves have disadvantage

### APPEARANCE DISADVANTAGES [Variable]

**DISTINCTIVE FEATURES [-5 CP]**
- Unusual but not frightening appearance
- Bright unnatural hair color, odd eyes, unusual markings
- Easily recognized and remembered
- -2 to Stealth in social situations
- **Examples:** Colored skin, glowing eyes, unusual height

**UNUSUAL APPEARANCE [-10 CP]**
- Clearly inhuman but not terrifying
- Blue/green skin, pointed ears, tail, small horns
- Cannot easily blend into normal society
- Disadvantage on Persuasion with normal humans
- **Examples:** Nightcrawler (blue skin, tail), Beast (blue fur)

**MONSTROUS APPEARANCE [-15 CP]**
- Frightening or disturbing to normal people
- Scales, fangs, claws always visible, hulking size
- Children cry, people cross street to avoid you
- Disadvantage on all Charisma checks with normal people except Intimidation
- May be attacked on sight in some areas
- **Examples:** Thing (rock body), Killer Croc, Hulk

**HORRIFIC APPEARANCE [-20 CP]**
- Terrifying to look at
- Rotting flesh, demonic features, made of insects/spiders
- Normal people flee or attack
- Advantage on Intimidation, disadvantage on all other Charisma checks
- Cannot go in public without disguise
- **Examples:** Zombies, demons, body horror characters

---

### PHYSICAL LIMITATIONS [Variable]

**CANNOT FLY [-15 CP]**
- Permanently grounded even though you should be able to fly
- Only take this if it's unusual for your type (Paragon who can't fly, wingless dragon, etc.)
- **Examples:** Juggernaut class characters in setting where flight is common

**SLOW MOVEMENT [-10 CP]**
- Base walking speed is only 20 ft
- Cannot take Dash action
- Always move last in initiative ties

**FRAGILE [-15 CP]**
- Unusually brittle or delicate
- Maximum HP is reduced by 20%
- Vulnerability to bludgeoning damage
- **Examples:** Elderly characters, those with brittle bone disease

**WEAK [-10 CP]**
- Below-average strength
- -2 to all STR-based checks
- Carrying capacity halved
- Cannot use Heavy weapons effectively

## MENTAL/BEHAVIORAL DISADVANTAGES

### PERSONALITY FLAWS [Variable]

**OVERCONFIDENCE [-5 CP]**
- Believe you can handle any situation
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to retreat from combat or back down from challenge
- Advantage on Intimidation, disadvantage on Insight (don't recognize when outmatched)
- **Examples:** Tony Stark, Namor, most Paragons

**COWARDICE [-10 CP]**
- Easily frightened
- Disadvantage on all saves against fear
- Must make WIS save DC 10 to enter obvious danger
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to stand and fight when bloodied
- **Examples:** Shaggy (Scooby-Doo), cowardly characters

**BAD TEMPER [-10 CP]**
- Quick to anger
- When provoked or insulted, must make WIS save DC 12 or become enraged
- While enraged, must attack or verbally assault the provoker
- Lasts 1 minute or until provoker apologizes
- **Examples:** Wolverine, Thing, short-tempered characters

**BERSERK [-15 CP]**
- Enter uncontrollable combat rage
- When you take damage in combat, roll d20
- On 5 or less, enter berserk rage
- While berserk: Must attack nearest creature (friend or foe) with melee attacks
- Cannot use items, cast spells, or take defensive actions
- Lasts until combat ends or you're unconscious
- **Examples:** Berserkers, rage-prone warriors

**COMPULSIVE BEHAVIOR [-5 to -15 CP]**
- Must perform specific behavior regularly
- **Minor (tell jokes, gamble) [-5 CP]:** Annoying but rarely dangerous
- **Moderate (lie, steal small items) [-10 CP]:** Gets you in trouble
- **Severe (kill, destroy) [-15 CP]:** Seriously dangerous
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to resist compulsion
- Failure increases to DC 15 for rest of day
- **Examples:** Trickster (must play pranks), kleptomaniac, pyromaniac

**OBSESSION [-10 CP]**
- Fixated on specific goal, person, or object
- Will pursue obsession even at personal cost
- Make WIS save DC 14 to prioritize anything else over obsession
- **Examples:** 
  - Punisher (kill criminals)
  - Batman (justice for Gotham)
  - Lex Luthor (destroy Superman)
  - Ahab (kill Moby Dick)

**PACIFISM [-15 CP]**
- Cannot bring yourself to kill or seriously harm
- Cannot make attacks intended to reduce creature to 0 HP
- Can use non-lethal force only
- Make WIS save DC 16 to break pacifism even in dire circumstances
- **Examples:** Aang (Avatar), peaceful characters

**BLOODLUST [-10 CP]**
- Struggle to stop fighting and leave enemies alive
- When enemy is at 0 HP, must make WIS save DC 12 to stop attacking them
- When in combat, must make WIS save DC 12 to accept surrender
- **Examples:** Wolverine (when feral), Lobo, brutal warriors

**GREED [-10 CP]**
- Compelled to acquire wealth and valuables
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to pass up obvious treasure
- Must make WIS save DC 14 to spend money on anything but yourself
- Will take unnecessary risks for treasure

**LECHEROUS [-10 CP]**
- Inappropriate behavior toward attractive people
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to not flirt with attractive people at inappropriate times
- Disadvantage on Insight when dealing with attractive people
- **Examples:** James Bond, flirtatious rogues

**SADISM [-10 CP]**
- Enjoy inflicting pain and suffering
- Prefer to draw out combat and make enemies suffer
- Must make WIS save DC 14 to show mercy or end fight quickly
- Often torture enemies for information even when unnecessary
- **Examples:** Carnage, Joker, evil characters

**TRUTHFULNESS [-5 CP]**
- Cannot lie, even by omission
- Must make WIS save DC 14 to tell even white lies
- Can refuse to answer questions
- Advantage on Persuasion (people know you're honest)

**HONESTY [-10 CP]**
- Will not steal, cheat, break the law, or lie
- Refuse to commit crimes even if necessary
- Must make WIS save DC 16 to break the law for good reason
- May turn in party members for crimes
- **Examples:** Lawful stupid characters, paladin stereotypes

### PHOBIAS [Variable]

Fear of specific thing or situation.
- When encountering phobia trigger, must make WIS save
- **Severity:**
  - **Mild [-5 CP] (DC 10):** Disadvantage on checks while near trigger
  - **Severe [-10 CP] (DC 14):** Cannot willingly approach trigger, paralyzed if forced
  - **Extreme [-15 CP] (DC 18):** Must flee in terror, incapacitated if can't flee

**Common Phobias:**
- **Acrophobia:** Heights
- **Arachnophobia:** Spiders
- **Claustrophobia:** Enclosed spaces (very common)
- **Nyctophobia:** Darkness
- **Pyrophobia:** Fire
- **Hydrophobia:** Water/drowning
- **Agoraphobia:** Crowds/open spaces
- **Hemophobia:** Blood
- **Thanatophobia:** Death

**Examples:**
- Storm (severe claustrophobia) [-10 CP]
- Indiana Jones (mild ophidiophobia - snakes) [-5 CP]

### MENTAL INSTABILITY [Variable]

**SPLIT PERSONALITY [-15 CP]**
- Have second (or more) distinct personality
- Roll d20 at start of each session or when stressed
- On 5 or less, other personality takes over
- While other personality controls: Different alignment, goals, memories
- May not remember what other personality did
- **Examples:** 
  - Moon Knight (Marc Spector, Steven Grant, Jake Lockley)
  - Hulk/Banner (early versions)
  - Legion

**PTSD (Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder) [-10 CP]**
- Suffer from traumatic past event
- When triggered by reminder (GM determines), must make WIS save DC 14
- Failure: Flashback (incapacitated for 1 minute) or panic attack (disadvantage on all rolls for 10 minutes)
- Nightmares prevent full rest on failed DC 12 WIS save
- **Examples:** 
  - Frank Castle (Punisher - family murdered)
  - Jessica Jones (Purple Man torture)
  - Captain America (lost in ice)
  - Winter Soldier (brainwashing)

**PARANOIA [-10 CP]**
- Distrust everyone and see threats everywhere
- Disadvantage on Insight (see threats that aren't there)
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to trust anyone
- Must make WIS save DC 14 to accept help from others
- **Examples:** Rorschach, conspiracy theorists

**DELUSION [-5 to -15 CP]**
- Believe something that isn't true
- **Mild [-5 CP]:** Minor false belief (think you're more important/skilled than you are)
- **Moderate [-10 CP]:** Significant false belief (think you're character from history, deny obvious truth)
- **Severe [-15 CP]:** Major break from reality (think you're god, deny your own identity)
- **Examples:** 
  - Don Quixote (thinks he's knight) [-15 CP]
  - Deadpool (thinks he's in comic book) [-10 CP, also breaks 4th wall for fun]

**AMNESIA [-10 CP]**
- Don't remember your past
- No memories before character creation
- May have skills but don't know how you got them
- Searching for your identity is ongoing quest
- **Examples:** Jason Bourne, Wolverine (at times)

### SOCIAL LIMITATIONS [Variable]

**LONER [-5 CP]**
- Work poorly in teams
- -1 to checks when using Help action or being helped
- Prefer to work alone even when teamwork is better option
- Must make WIS save DC 10 to follow team plan instead of going alone
- **Examples:** Wolverine, Batman (sometimes), solo operators

**INTOLERANCE [-5 CP]**
- Prejudiced against specific group (race, religion, nationality, etc.)
- -2 to reaction rolls with that group
- Disadvantage on Persuasion with them
- Must make WIS save DC 12 to work with them peacefully
- **Examples:** Racists, religious fanatics

**FANATICISM [-10 CP]**
- Devoted to cause, religion, or leader beyond reason
- Will not question your cause
- Will die for your cause if ordered
- Must make WIS save DC 16 to act against cause's interests
- **Examples:** Zealots, cultists, brainwashed minions

## SUPERNATURAL/POWER DISADVANTAGES

### WEAKNESSES [Variable]

**SUBSTANCE WEAKNESS [-15 to -30 CP]**
- Specific material causes you harm
- **Rarity determines value:**
  - **Very Rare (Kryptonite, unicorn horn) [-15 CP]**
  - **Uncommon (silver, cold iron, salt) [-20 CP]**
  - **Common (wood, water, sunlight) [-30 CP]**
- When exposed to substance:
  - Take 2d6 damage per round of contact
  - Lose access to all powers/advantages
  - Disadvantage on all rolls
- Effects last until substance removed and you heal
- **Examples:**
  - Superman (Kryptonite) [-15 CP]
  - Werewolves (silver) [-20 CP]
  - Vampires (sunlight, wood, garlic) [-30 CP each, usually combined]
  - Venom (fire, sonics) [-20 CP each]

**ELEMENTAL VULNERABILITY [-10 to -20 CP]**
- Take extra damage from specific damage type
- **×1.5 damage [-10 CP]**
- **×2 damage [-15 CP]**
- **×3 damage [-20 CP]**
- Common types: Fire, Cold, Lightning, Acid, Psychic, Magic, Radiant, Necrotic
- **Examples:**
  - Thing (none, tough)
  - Venom (fire ×2, sonic ×2) [-15 CP each]
  - Plant creatures (fire ×1.5) [-10 CP]
  - Trolls (fire and acid ×2) [-15 CP each]

**MAGIC VULNERABILITY [-20 CP]**
- No resistance to magical effects
- Magic bypasses your DR
- Disadvantage on saves vs magic
- Magical attacks have advantage against you
- **Examples:** Superman, technology-based characters

**SONIC VULNERABILITY [-15 CP]**
- Sound-based attacks deal extra damage (×2)
- Loud noises can stun you (CON save DC 14 or stunned for 1 round)
- Disadvantage on saves vs thunder damage
- **Examples:** Venom, Carnage, symbiotes

---

### POWER LIMITATIONS [Variable]

**POWER INCONTINENCE [-10 CP]**
- Powers activate uncontrollably at random
- When stressed (GM determines), roll d20
- On 5 or less, random power activates at full strength
- You don't choose target or effect
- Can be dangerous to allies
- **Examples:** 
  - Rogue (can't turn off absorption touch)
  - Cyclops (eyes always firing unless visor)
  - Young mutants learning control

**POWER INSTABILITY [-15 CP]**
- Power strength fluctuates unpredictably
- Whenever you use power, roll d20:
  - 1-5: Half power/effect
  - 6-15: Normal
  - 16-20: Double power/effect (may cause collateral damage)
- **Examples:** Jubilee (unstable fireworks), inexperienced heroes

**POWER BURNOUT [-20 CP]**
- Using powers too much causes exhaustion
- After using powers 5+ times in one combat:
  - Make CON save DC 12
  - Failure: Gain 1 level of exhaustion
  - DC increases by +2 for each additional use
- **Examples:** Cable (constant power use fighting techno-organic virus)

**POWER LOSS [-20 to -40 CP]**
- Lose access to powers under specific conditions
- **Uncommon condition (in darkness, when wet) [-20 CP]**
- **Common condition (when emotional, when not touching earth) [-30 CP]**
- **Very common condition (during daytime, when not angry) [-40 CP]**
- While powers lost: Lose all advantages, become normal human
- **Examples:**
  - Green Lantern (ring runs out of charge)
  - Aquaman (weaker on land in some versions)
  - Hulk (reverts to Banner when calm)

**FEEDBACK [-10 CP]**
- When powers are resisted, countered, or reflected, you take damage
- When save succeeds against your power: Take 1d6 psychic damage
- When power is dispelled/countered: Take 2d6 psychic damage
- When power is reflected back: Take your own power's damage
- **Examples:** Psychics, magic users with mental link to powers

**COLLATERAL DAMAGE [-15 CP]**
- Your powers always affect more than intended
- Area attacks are 50% larger radius
- Single-target attacks also damage adjacent squares
- Cannot use powers without risking bystanders
- -2 to all Stealth (your powers are obvious and destructive)
- **Examples:** Cyclops (optic blasts destroy everything), Havok

**UNCONTROLLABLE POWER [-15 CP]**
- Specific power cannot be turned off
- Choose one of your powers - it's always active
- May need to wear special gear to contain it
- **Examples:**
  - Rogue (absorption touch always on)
  - Cyclops (eyes always firing without ruby quartz visor)
  - Rouge (can't touch anyone skin-to-skin)

**LIMITED USE [-10 CP per major limitation]**
- Specific power only works X times per day
- Choose power worth 30+ CP
- It only works 1-3 times per day (you choose when creating disadvantage)
- **Examples:** Ultimate attacks, transformation powers

**REQUIRES SPECIFIC CONDITION [-10 to -30 CP]**
- Powers only work when specific condition is met
- **Uncommon (full moon, solar eclipse) [-10 CP]**
- **Common (in sunlight, while raining) [-20 CP]**
- **Very common (while angry, when touching ground) [-30 CP]**
- Powers completely non-functional outside condition
- **Examples:**
  - Werewolves (full moon transformation)
  - Solar-powered characters
  - Rage-based transformations

---

### TRANSFORMATION ISSUES [Variable]

**UNCONTROLLED TRANSFORMATION [-20 CP]**
- Transform involuntarily under specific trigger
- Choose trigger: When angry, at night, when stressed, etc.
- No control over when you transform
- May not remember what you did while transformed
- **Examples:**
  - Hulk (transforms when angry)
  - Werewolves (full moon)
  - Jekyll/Hyde

**STUCK IN FORM [-15 CP]**
- Difficult or impossible to return to natural form
- Permanently transformed, or can only revert with great difficulty (magic, technology, rare conditions)
- Must live with alternate appearance/abilities
- **Examples:**
  - Thing (permanently rock, can't turn back)
  - Beast (permanently blue and furry)
  - Those cursed into forms

**MONSTROUS ALTERNATE FORM [-10 CP]**
- Your alternate form is frightening or disturbing
- While transformed: Count as having Monstrous Appearance
- People fear and flee from you
- **Examples:**
  - Hulk (giant green rage monster)
  - Beast (when first transformed)
  - Monster transformations

## SOCIAL DISADVANTAGES

### IDENTITY/REPUTATION [Variable]

**SECRET IDENTITY [-10 CP]**
- Must protect civilian identity from discovery
- If exposed:
  - Loved ones targeted by enemies
  - Lose job, social standing
  - Hunted by authorities or villains
- Must maintain dual life (time and resource drain)
- **Examples:** Most superheroes (Spider-Man, Batman, etc.)

**HUNTED [-10 to -30 CP]**
- Someone or something actively seeks you
- **Single enemy (rival, one villain) [-10 CP]**
  - Appears occasionally (1-2 times per story arc)
  - Equal power level
- **Organization (government, crime syndicate) [-20 CP]**
  - Appears frequently (once per session or more)
  - Well-funded and organized
- **Powerful force (cosmic entity, god) [-30 CP]**
  - Can appear at any time
  - Far more powerful than you
- **Examples:**
  - Mutants (hunted by Sentinels) [-20 CP]
  - Fugitives from future
  - Those who stole something valuable

**WANTED [-15 CP]**
- Authorities actively seek your arrest
- Wanted posters, APBs, bounties
- Cannot go to authorities for help
- If captured, face trial and imprisonment
- **Examples:**
  - Vigilantes who kill (Punisher)
  - Former criminals
  - Framed heroes

**MISTAKEN FOR VILLAIN [-10 CP]**
- People fear and hate you despite good intentions
- Media portrays you as threat
- Must make DC 14 Persuasion check for people to trust you
- Authorities may attack on sight
- **Examples:**
  - Hulk (seen as monster)
  - Venom (looks like villain)
  - Misunderstood heroes

**BAD REPUTATION [-10 CP]**
- Known for past failures, crimes, or behavior
- -2 to all social interactions with those who know your reputation
- Disadvantage on Persuasion
- People don't trust you
- **Examples:**
  - Reformed villains
  - Heroes who failed publicly
  - Those with checkered pasts

**INFAMOUS [-15 CP]**
- Everyone knows your crimes or failures
- Cannot go anywhere without being recognized
- -4 to social interactions
- May be attacked or refused service
- **Examples:**
  - Well-known villains trying to reform
  - Heroes who caused major disasters

### RESPONSIBILITIES [Variable]

**DUTY [-10 to -20 CP]**
- Obligated to organization or cause
- Must respond when called
- **Part-time (Avengers, X-Men) [-10 CP]**
  - Called on occasionally
  - Missions 1-2 per month
  - Can refuse in emergencies
- **Full-time (SHIELD agent, professional hero) [-15 CP]**
  - Regular missions every week
  - Expected to prioritize duty
  - Salary may be provided
- **Extreme (constantly on call, military) [-20 CP]**
  - Can be called any time
  - Must drop everything
  - Severe consequences for refusal
- **Examples:**
  - Avengers members [-10 CP]
  - SHIELD agents [-15 CP]
  - Military superheroes [-20 CP]

**DEPENDENTS [-5 CP per person]**
- Must protect loved ones who are vulnerable
- Family members, sidekicks, civilians you feel responsible for
- Enemies can target them to get to you
- Must spend time/resources protecting them
- **Examples:**
  - Spider-Man (Aunt May, MJ) [-10 CP]
  - Superman (Lois Lane, Ma and Pa Kent) [-15 CP]
  - Batman (Robin, Alfred) [-10 CP]

**CODE OF HONOR [-10 to -15 CP]**
- Must follow personal code of conduct
- **Hero's Code [-10 CP]:**
  - Don't kill
  - Protect innocents
  - Keep your word
  - Fight fair when possible
- **Warrior's Code [-10 CP]:**
  - Only fight worthy opponents
  - Honor combat challenges
  - Show respect to defeated foes
- **Extreme Code [-15 CP]:**
  - Never lie
  - Never use powers for personal gain
  - Never refuse challenge
- Violating code causes guilt, loss of powers, or other consequences
- **Examples:**
  - Superman (Hero's Code)
  - Captain America (Hero's Code)
  - Thor (Warrior's Code)

**VOW [-5 to -15 CP]**
- Sworn promise or oath you must keep
- **Minor (never drink alcohol, always help elderly) [-5 CP]**
- **Major (protect specific location, never kill) [-10 CP]**
- **Extreme (never use powers offensively, poverty vow) [-15 CP]**
- Breaking vow has severe consequences (loss of powers, divine punishment, etc.)
- **Examples:**
  - Monks (poverty, chastity vows)
  - Paladins (oaths)
  - Characters bound by magic oaths

**SENSE OF DUTY [-5 to -15 CP]**
- Feel responsible for specific group
- Will risk self to protect them
- **Friends/teammates [-5 CP]**
- **Community/neighborhood [-10 CP]**
- **All humanity [-15 CP]**
- Must make WIS save DC 16 to ignore cry for help from those you protect
- **Examples:**
  - Spider-Man (neighborhood) [-10 CP]
  - Superman (all humanity) [-15 CP]

### SOCIAL STIGMA [Variable]

**MINORITY [-5 CP]**
- Member of discriminated-against group
- Face prejudice in some areas
- -1 to social checks in prejudiced regions
- May be denied service or opportunities
- **Examples:** Various real-world minorities, some alien species

**CRIMINAL [-10 CP]**
- Convicted felon with criminal record
- Restricted rights (can't vote, own firearms, travel freely)
- Difficulty finding employment
- Police hassle you regularly
- **Examples:** Ex-cons, paroled villains

**MUTANT/METAHUMAN (in anti-meta setting) [-10 CP]**
- Feared and hated for having powers
- Face discrimination, violence, bigotry
- Hate groups target you
- May be required to register with government
- **Examples:** X-Men (Marvel), metas in some DC settings

**MONSTER [-15 CP]**
- Seen as inhuman, dangerous creature
- Normal people flee or attack on sight
- No legal rights or protections
- Hunted by authorities and civilians
- **Examples:**
  - Vampires, werewolves (in some settings)
  - Frankenstein's monster
  - Visible monsters

**SECOND-CLASS CITIZEN [-10 CP]**
- Legally restricted in rights and freedoms
- Cannot own property, vote, testify in court, etc.
- May need permits to exist in public
- **Examples:**
  - Robots/AI in some settings
  - Mutants under Sentinel program
  - Aliens in xenophobic societies

### ENEMY/RIVAL DISADVANTAGES [Variable]

**ENEMY [-5 to -30 CP]**
- Someone actively opposes you with intent to harm
- **Calculate CP value:**
  - **Frequency:**
    - Rare (1/story arc): ×1
    - Occasional (1/session): ×2
    - Frequent (2+/session): ×3
  - **Power Level:**
    - Weaker than you: -5 CP base
    - Equal to you: -10 CP base
    - Stronger than you: -20 CP base
  - **Multiply frequency × power level**
- **Examples:**
  - Spider-Man's Green Goblin (frequent, equal): -20 CP
  - Batman's Joker (occasional, equal): -20 CP
  - Superman's Lex Luthor (occasional, weaker but resourceful): -10 CP
  - X-Men vs Sentinels (frequent, stronger): -60 CP (organization, not single enemy)

**RIVAL [-5 CP]**
- Someone competes with you
- Not actively harmful but causes problems
- Tries to one-up you, steal glory
- May help you against greater threats
- **Examples:**
  - Thing vs Human Torch (friendly rivalry)
  - Heroes competing for fame
  - Sibling rivalry

## DESTINY/CURSE DISADVANTAGES [Variable]

**DESTINY (Dire Prophecy) [-20 to -40 CP]**
- Fated for specific terrible outcome
- **Major prophecy (will betray friends, cause disaster) [-20 CP]**
- **World-ending prophecy (will destroy world, become great evil) [-40 CP]**
- Cannot avoid destiny, only delay it
- Source of constant dread and story hooks
- **Examples:**
  - Hellboy (destined to end world)
  - Anakin Skywalker (become Darth Vader)
  - Greek tragic heroes

**CURSED [-15 to -30 CP]**
- Suffer from magical or divine curse
- **Moderate curse (bad luck, transformation trigger) [-15 CP]**
  - Minor ongoing effects
  - Can sometimes be worked around
- **Severe curse (loved ones die, powers hurt you) [-30 CP]**
  - Major life impact
  - Difficult or impossible to remove
- **Examples:**
  - Those who anger gods
  - Cursed objects bonded to you
  - Family curses

**SHORT LIFESPAN [-20 CP]**
- Will die young (known fact)
- Live only 5-10 years from character creation
- Terminal illness, genetic condition, curse, etc.
